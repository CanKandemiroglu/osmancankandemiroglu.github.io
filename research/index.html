Research

Four projects, one molecular question.

Each of these projects took a different toolkit — lipids, isotopes, high-resolution mass spectrometry — into a different environment. Together they sketch out what marine molecular biogeochemistry can do.

Field & lab · Black Sea Paleobiogeochemistry

Archaeal GDGTs in a stratified, anoxic basin

Reading archaeal membrane lipids from a stratified anoxic basin to reconstruct past ocean surface-water temperature.

GDGT TEX86 Thaumarchaeota Paleoclimate

Lab · Helgoland Mud Area

13C-acetate incubations in coastal sediments

Following 13C from acetate into microbial lipids in North Sea muds, to identify which organisms turn over small carbon substrates.

Lipid-SIP Acetate Sediment biogeochemistry North Sea

Open project

Field & imaging · Mallorca

Microbial mats & MALDI-FT-ICR-MS imaging

Layered hypersaline mats imaged at ultra-high mass resolution — and what they suggest about life in Mars-analogue habitats.
