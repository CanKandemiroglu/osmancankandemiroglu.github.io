Marine molecular biogeochemist and geophysical engineer who combines high-resolution organic mass spectrometry (FT-ICR-MS, MALDI/LDI imaging, GC-MS, HPLC-MS) and lipid-biomarker analysis with a hydrothermal-systems background and hands-on experimental instrument-building. I am eager to learn LILBID and impact-ionization mass spectrometry to detect lipids and fatty acids in ice grains and to trace organics from Enceladus's hydrothermal vent systems into the plume — the aim of SFB 1759 subproject C01 (Enceladus). As a trained geophysicist (seismic-signal interpretation) and an engineer who builds experimental systems from scratch, I am as comfortable on the instrument and physics side as on the molecular side.

Core methods & techniques

High-resolution mass spectrometry
FT-ICR-MS; MALDI/LDI-FT-ICR MS imaging (Bruker solariX XR); GC-MS; HPLC-MS (APCI); GC-IRMS / compound-specific stable-isotope analysis
Experimental & cultivation biogeochemistry
Marine bacterial cultivation (Vibrio spp.); long-term & anaerobic incubations; ¹³C stable-isotope probing; alginate model-particle synthesis (emulsification / internal gelation); building experimental set-ups from scratch
Lipid-biomarker & organic geochemistry
Total lipid extraction (ultrasonication, centrifugation); open-column liquid chromatography; derivatisation / silylation; hydrocarbons, sterols, GDGTs, intact polar lipids, hopanoids, alkenones, pigments / carotenoids, quinones
Microscale imaging & geochemical mapping
MS imaging (molecular mapping); µ-XRF elemental mapping; microsensor profiling (O₂, H₂S, pH, redox); time-lapse microscopy
Aqueous & instrumental (earlier training)
Aqueous geochemical modelling (PhreeqC, AquaChem); ICP-MS, GF-AAS, FE-SEM–EDX; borehole geophysical logging (full-waveform sonic, acoustic televiewer, gamma-ray)
